You have an account regarded as defaulted or delinquent

Defaulted and delinquent accounts can be viewed in the Account Information section of your credit report.An account is in default when the terms of a credit agreement have not been met and the agreement has ended and is indicated by a status history of 8.An account is considered delinquent when payments in the last 12 months of activity are significantly late. These late payments can be indicated by a status 4 or more or by two or more consecutive status 3 entries.As the default or delinquent account becomes older, most lenders are likely to view you as lower risk.

» More details in your full credit report

You have recently opened 1 or more new credit accounts.

Opening multiple accounts in a short period can indicate that you are credit hungry and that your existing credit is not sufficient for your needs. The more accounts that you open in a 6 month period, the more lenders are likely to view you as high risk. As the age of your accounts increases, the more positively lenders are likely to view you.

» More details in your full credit report

The age of your accounts indicates lenders are likely to view you as higher risk

The age of each account is calculated from the start date.Having a lower average age on your accounts shows that lenders may be unable to see how effective you are at managing credit. As the average age of your accounts increases, the more positively lenders are likely to view you.

» More details in your full credit report

The usage of your available credit indicates a higher risk

Having high Credit Limit Utilisation (balance compared to limit) shows that you need to use a large proportion of your available credit and you may also be having difficulty increasing this available credit.Lower utilisation is more likely to be viewed as lower risk by most lenders.

» More details in your full credit report

You have no recent searches on your credit report

Having no recent searches (applications for credit) shows that you are not credit hungry and that your existing credit may be sufficient for your needs. The more searches that you have in a 6 month period, the more likely a lender will consider you to be higher risk.

Credit History Summary

Credit history summary

Here's a summary of your most up to date credit report. Check it and your score regularly to keep updated on the status of your credit.

Number of credit accounts (including those settled)7 

Credit accounts

Credit accounts will include such things as credit cards, bank accounts, store cards, loans and mobile phone contracts. Settled accounts are where the credit has been fully repaid. You should regularly check your full credit report for unfamiliar accounts as a precaution against ID fraud.
 
Amount of available credit (excluding mortgages) £431 

Available credit

Available credit is what you have left after your total borrowing has been deducted from your total credit limit (excluding mortgages).

For example, if you were using £300 of a £700 credit card limit, providing this is your only source of credit, your available credit would be stated at £400.
 
% of your total credit being used (excluding mortgages) 82% 

Total credit usage

If lenders think that you're at the limit of what you can afford, they may not want to grant you more credit. Using a large percentage of your total credit each month, even if you make all your repayments on time may have a negative impact on how lenders assess you.
 
Amount of outstanding debt (excluding mortgages and defaults) £2,046 

Outstanding debt

Outstanding debt is the amount you owe on your existing credit agreements (excluding mortgages and defaults).
 
Number of accounts overdue 0 

Accounts overdue

An account overdue is one where you have late payments recorded. It is important to ensure you are up to date on all of your account payments to help you take control of your financial affairs. You should regularly check your full report for any suspicious overdue accounts as a precaution against ID fraud.
 
Number of missed or late repayments in the last year 0 

Missed or late payments

Missed or late repayments will stay on your credit report for at least 36 months and can seriously damage your credit rating. If you are struggling to keep up with your repayments let the lenders know you're having problems and they may be able to help you work out a schedule of payments you can afford.
